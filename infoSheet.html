This study is investigating audio algorithms designed to improve how individuals localise where a sound comes from when using binaural technology. We are inviting participants who are:

  • normal hearing
  • 18 years or older

to participate in this study. You may terminate your participation at any time.

What tasks will I be asked to complete?

This App has five parts that you will be guided through. These are:

  • Infomation Sheet - Reading and indicating if you agree or disagree to participate.
  • Experiment Settings - Generating a unique ID and setting your headphones to a comfortable level.
  • Calibration - Complete a short process to generate a unique audio algorithm.
  • Training - to introduce different parts of the interface.
  • Listening Test - The full listening test, this involves short noise bursts and identifying on a diagram the direction you perceived them as coming from.

After completing the listening test you will need to upload the results.

Are there any risks involved?

Participation in this study is low risk, with noise exposure and fatigue the only possible risks. To mitigate the risk of noise exposure, the app will guide you through the process setting the audio level to a safe volume before being allowed to continue.

To minimise the risk of fatigue, the app will prompt you to take a break part way through. You are reminded your participation in this experiment is voluntary and you may pause the experiment to take a rest or stop the experiment entirely at any time.

By completing the tasks in this app you will be contributing to research at the University of York. This means that you consent to us using your responses for the research purposes outlined below. All data is collected anonymously and is stored confidentially in accordance with UK and EU Data Protection laws.

Anonymous responses to the survey and experimental task will be stored in line with the University policy. They may be used (either individually or aggregated across participants) in internal or external research publications and/or to inform the design and implementation of new audio technology with our commercial partners.

Results from the app will only be stored if you choose to upload them.

If you wish to receive a copy of your data or withdraw your data, you can do this by contacting ncab500@york.ac.uk or gavin.kearney@york.ac.uk
